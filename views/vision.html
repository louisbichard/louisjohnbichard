Vision

What the industry needs, how I can provide it & where I want to be.


The problem



The internet has brought about profound changes in the way we live. Technical innovations are creating products for problems we didn’t even know we had. Any idea that can be converted into technology already is and new and profitable ideas are now only generated by those with a deep understanding of user problems; smart creatives as Google likes to call these people.

Speed to market and speed to test product hypothesises means that products are competing more ferociously and in order to differentiate businesses need to validate their ideas quicker to bring more valuable products to market.

New technologies are constantly evolving, meaning that for software engineers the volume of technical “options” is very hard to keep on-top of. To be effective technical decision makers and influencers of these decisions have to be able to understand what is the correct tool for the job. But with so many tools, it can be hard to make these decisions in an informed manner.


The solution



I believe taking responsibility for the full end to end process is what helps to create good software (cross-functionality as scrum would define it). Extending the responsibility of the software engineer to include business awareness on topics such as: product KPI’s, user adoption, revenue model etc gives more meaning to the software being created.

The engineer understands the user and can deliver technical solutions, not just the product owner, not just the public facing sales team, but the software engineers themselves. Software engineers who balance both technical and business understanding are the most desirable in markets creating new and innovative products. Software engineers then understand not only what can be done, but what should be done.



My values


What I believe in, no matter what I'm working on or where


Moral

A pragmatic approach to ensuring morality in my work is very important to me. Doing what is right for the customers of the product i’m building, ensuring the welfare of my colleagues and not abusing the trust that others place in me.

Analytical

By nature I am extremely analytical. I want to understand problems at their deepest level. This ranges from studying psychology of habit forming products to understanding how the agile principles can be implemented practically to deliver value.

Holistic

My USP. Augmenting software engineering skills with free thought allow me to bring together a unique blend that is extremely value to businesses wanting to build quality software products. As a result I’m a strong cross-functional / scrum advocate and believe knowledge breadth is imperative.

Efficient

Being an enthusiast I believe there is no problem that cannot be solved with technology. I am not afraid to experiment to find novel ways of increasing my own effectiveness This is why my mouse runs at 10x usual speed, or why I can be seen on a kneeling chair from time to time.



Career vision


Where I want to work; Who I want to become


Environments I thrive in

I enjoy working in innovative environments and once that embrace change and are not dogmatic in their approach.

Businesses that understand that delivering value is the most important, not what social norms they should put in place.

I consider myself pragmatic and hope to work with those that relate to this too, doing what is required to get the job done at times, but getting it right when it counts, this is a hard balance but it's extremely important.

My future self

I would like to be a technical leader in as many different areas of software, from the soft skills such as understanding how to run a good design / requirements gathering session to more hard technical skills such as how to best organise an X application and understanding what tools I'd need to do this.

I will achieve this by continually pushing myself to keep up with the latest technologies, and by ensuring that I have a working knowledge in as many domains as possible.
